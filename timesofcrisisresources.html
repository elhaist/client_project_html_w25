Mental Health Resources

Refer to these resources for any mental health realted topics.

Counseling and Psychological Services (CAPS)

CAPS is offered through the University Health & Counseling (UHC) department at the University of Michigan.

CAPS offers a variety of services to students. CAPS offers non-emergency and emergency services.

Some of the non-emergency services resources offered by CAPS include:

  • Individual Peer Counseling
  • Teletherapy
  • Wellness Coaching
  • Wellness Zones

Some of the non-emergency services resources offered by CAPS include:

  • CAPS Crisis Response Team
  • Sexual Assault Services
  • Sucicide Prevention Services

Food Pantry Resources

The University of Michigan provides free access to the Maize and Blue Cupboard (campus food pantry) to all students.

To visit the Maize and Blue Cupboard, students just need to make a prior appointment.

Make an apointment through their sign-up page.

The food pantry provides students with much more than food!

Along with pantry items, the food pantry provides

  • Kitchen and Dishware
  • Personal Hygiene items
  • Cleaning Supplies
  • Resources to support students

Fun Fact!The Maize and Blue Cupboard also provides free recipes on their website to provide some inspiration!

Financial Help Resources

The University of Michigan provides numerous emergency funds, subsidy plans, and financial aid options for students facing financial difficulties.

Financial Aid Options

Given that there are less finanical aid options for graduate students, the university recommends that students refer to their school or college for financial aid inquiries.

MSI students should refer to the Tuition and Funding page from the School of Information.

Other options for finacial aid help include:

  • Work Study
  • Financial Assistance through FAFSA
  • Setting up a tuition payment plan
